French Onion Grilled Cheese

Description

Indulge in the ultimate comfort food with this timeless classic â€“ the Grilled Cheese Sandwich. This simple yet satisfying recipe combines the gooey goodness of melted cheese with the crunch of perfectly toasted bread. Ideal for a quick lunch or a cozy dinner, it's a dish that never fails to delight taste buds of all ages.

Ingredients

Yield: 2 servings

  • 4 tablespoond unsalted butter
  • 1 pound of yellow onions, peeled, halved and thinly sliced
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons sherry, red-wine or white-wine veinegar (optional)
  • 4 ounces Gruyere cheese, grated
  • 4 slices bread, cut no wider than 1/2-inch thick

Steps

  1. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, melt 2 tablespoons butter. Add the onions and season with salt and pepper. Cover and cook, stirring once or twice, until the onions are softened, 3 to 5 minutes.
  2. Uncover, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook, stirring occasionally, until deep golden brown, 20 to 25 minutes. If the onions look dry or like they might burn, add a few tablespoons of water at a time, scraping up any browned bits that are stuck to the bottom of the skillet.
  3. (Optional) Once the onions are done, deglaze the skillet with vinegar and cook until the liquid has evaporated, about 1 minute. Transfer the onions to a medium bowl and season to taste with salt and pepper. Wipe out the skillet, or wash it, if necessary.
  4. Add the cheese to the onions and stir to combine. Put down two slices of bread, and scoop half of the cheese-onion mixture onto each one. Top with the remaining slices of bread, and press down gently.
  5. In the skillet, melt 1 tablespoon butter over medium heat. Add the sandwiches and cook until the bottoms turn golden brown, 3 to 4 minutes, reducing the heat to prevent toast from darkening too quickly, if needed. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon butter, flip the sandwiches, press down and cook until the cheese has fully melted and the bottoms turn golden brown, 3 to 4 minutes. Serve immediately.
